# Comprehensive Cycling Weekly Training Schedules

This document provides a variety of weekly training schedule examples for cyclists, ranging from low volume to high volume, along with specific event focus templates and crucial guidelines for adaptation, recovery, and missed sessions.

## 1. Low Volume: FTP Focused (3 Days, 4-5 Hours/Week)
**Goal:** Maximize FTP and aerobic capacity with limited time. Focuses heavily on intensity due to low volume.
**Weekly TSS Target:** ~200-250

*   **Monday:** Rest
*   **Tuesday:** **VO2 Max / Threshold Intervals** (1.5h) - e.g., 5 x 4 min @ 105-110% FTP with 4 min rest. (TSS ~90)
*   **Wednesday:** Rest
*   **Thursday:** **Sweet Spot / Tempo** (1h) - e.g., 3 x 12 min @ 88-92% FTP with 3 min rest. (TSS ~65)
*   **Friday:** Rest
*   **Saturday:** **Endurance with Efforts** (2h) - Zone 2 (65-75% FTP) with 3-4 short sprints or 1 x 20 min Sweet Spot effort. (TSS ~80)
*   **Sunday:** Rest (or optional active recovery/stretching)

## 2. Mid Volume: Road Race Prep & FTP Improvement (5 Days, 6-8 Hours/Week)
**Goal:** Build race readiness, improve repeatable power, and push FTP higher. Balanced intensity and volume.
**Weekly TSS Target:** ~350-450

*   **Monday:** Rest
*   **Tuesday:** **Anaerobic / VO2 Max** (1.5h) - e.g., 6 x 3 min @ 110-115% FTP or 30/30s micro-intervals. (TSS ~100)
*   **Wednesday:** **Active Recovery or Light Endurance** (1h) - Zone 1/Low Zone 2 (50-60% FTP). (TSS ~30)
*   **Thursday:** **Threshold Intervals** (1.5h) - e.g., 4 x 10 min @ 95-100% FTP with 5 min rest. (TSS ~90)
*   **Friday:** Rest
*   **Saturday:** **Group Ride / Race Sim** (2-2.5h) - Unstructured but hard. Surges, attacking, sustained climbs. (TSS ~120-140)
*   **Sunday:** **Steady Endurance** (2h) - Zone 2 (65-75% FTP). Keep it steady, no surging. (TSS ~90)

## 3. High Volume Polarized (6 Days, 10-15 Hours/Week)
**Goal:** Maximize aerobic base and peak performance using an 80/20 intensity distribution (80% low intensity, 20% high intensity).
**Weekly TSS Target:** ~600-800+

*   **Monday:** Rest
*   **Tuesday:** **High Intensity (VO2 Max)** (1.5-2h) - e.g., 4 x 8 min @ 105% FTP. (TSS ~110)
*   **Wednesday:** **Long Endurance** (3h) - Strictly Zone 2 (65-70% FTP). (TSS ~140)
*   **Thursday:** **Endurance** (2h) - Strictly Zone 2 (65-70% FTP). (TSS ~90)
*   **Friday:** **High Intensity (Threshold/Sweet Spot)** (2h) - e.g., 2 x 20 min @ 95-100% FTP. (TSS ~120)
*   **Saturday:** **Long Endurance** (4h) - Strictly Zone 2. Optional fast group ride if polarized model is slightly relaxed. (TSS ~200)
*   **Sunday:** **Endurance / Recovery** (2h) - Low Zone 2 or Zone 1. (TSS ~70)

## 4. Hillclimb / Time Trial Focused (5 Days, 6-9 Hours/Week)
**Goal:** Maximize sustained power-to-weight ratio and lactate clearance. High focus on holding threshold and sweet spot.
**Weekly TSS Target:** ~400-500

*   **Monday:** Rest
*   **Tuesday:** **Over-Unders** (1.5h) - e.g., 3 x 12 min (2 min @ 95% FTP, 1 min @ 105% FTP). Train lactate clearance. (TSS ~95)
*   **Wednesday:** **Endurance** (1.5h) - Zone 2. (TSS ~70)
*   **Thursday:** **Sustained Threshold / Sweet Spot** (1.5h) - e.g., 3 x 15 min @ 90-95% FTP. Maintain aerodynamic position if TT focused. (TSS ~90)
*   **Friday:** Rest
*   **Saturday:** **Long Sustained Climbing** (2.5h) - Long blocks (20-40 mins) at Sweet Spot/Tempo on a local climb or Zwift. (TSS ~130)
*   **Sunday:** **Endurance** (2h) - Zone 2. (TSS ~90)

## 5. Maintenance / Fitness (3-4 Days, 4 Hours/Week)
**Goal:** Maintain current fitness level during the off-season or busy life periods.
**Weekly TSS Target:** ~150-200

*   **Monday:** Rest
*   **Tuesday:** **Sweet Spot** (1h) - e.g., 2 x 15 min @ 85-90% FTP. (TSS ~60)
*   **Wednesday:** Rest
*   **Thursday:** **Short Intervals** (45m) - e.g., 10 x 1 min hard, 1 min easy. Fun and punchy. (TSS ~50)
*   **Friday:** Rest
*   **Saturday:** **Endurance Ride** (1.5 - 2h) - Zone 2, enjoy the ride. (TSS ~70)
*   **Sunday:** Rest (or cross-training/walking)

## 6. Recovery Week Template
**Goal:** Shed fatigue while maintaining fitness. Volume is cut by ~40-50%, intensity is reduced but not entirely removed to keep the legs "open." Apply every 3rd or 4th week.

*   **Monday:** Rest
*   **Tuesday:** **Active Recovery** (45m) - Zone 1. (TSS ~20)
*   **Wednesday:** **Endurance with Openers** (1h) - Zone 2 with 3 x 30-second hard efforts (not all-out) just to clear the system. (TSS ~45)
*   **Thursday:** Rest
*   **Friday:** **Active Recovery** (45m) - Zone 1. (TSS ~20)
*   **Saturday:** **Endurance** (1.5h) - Low Zone 2. (TSS ~60)
*   **Sunday:** Rest

## 7. Return-From-Break Protocols
Returning to training requires patience. Ramp up slowly to avoid injury and burnout.

*   **1 Week Off (Illness/Vacation):**
    *   *Approach:* No major fitness lost. Start with 2 days of easy Zone 1/2 riding. Resume normal training by the weekend. Do not try to "make up" missed volume.
*   **2 Weeks Off:**
    *   *Approach:* Minor top-end fitness lost. First week back should be entirely Zone 2 endurance. Second week, reintroduce one Sweet Spot/Tempo workout. Third week, resume normal plan (consider re-testing FTP).
*   **1 Month Off:**
    *   *Approach:* Significant high-end fitness lost; base slightly detrained. Spend 2 full weeks doing only Zone 2 and light Tempo. Week 3 add one Threshold session. Lower FTP expectation by 5-10%.
*   **2+ Months Off:**
    *   *Approach:* Treat as a new season. Do a 4-week base building block (mostly Zone 2). Retest FTP before starting any interval plans. FTP will likely be down 10-20%. Focus on consistency, not power numbers.

## 8. How to Handle Missed Sessions
Life happens. Here is how to adjust when you miss a workout:
*   **Missed 1 Day (Weekday Interval):** Skip it. Do not double up the next day. Move on to the next scheduled workout.
*   **Missed 2-3 Days:** Shift the missed key interval session to your long weekend ride slot (replacing a portion of the long ride). Scrap the secondary interval session.
*   **Missed a Long Weekend Ride:** You cannot cram 4 hours of endurance into a 1-hour weekday slot. Let it go. Focus on hitting your intervals during the week to maintain stimulus.
*   **Rule of Thumb:** Never sacrifice rest days to make up for missed workouts. Doing so compromises recovery and ruins the quality of subsequent workouts.

## 9. Double-Day Training Guidance
Double days are for advanced riders (High Volume) seeking marginal gains or adapting to extreme schedules.
*   **Format A (AM Intensity, PM Recovery):** Morning session is high intensity (e.g., VO2 Max intervals). Evening session is 30-45 mins of active recovery (Zone 1) to flush legs.
*   **Format B (Commuter Style):** AM and PM are both Zone 2 (e.g., 1 hour to work, 1 hour home). Excellent for building base volume.
*   **Nutrition:** Glycogen replenishment between sessions is critical. Consume carbohydrates immediately after the first session.
*   **Warning:** Monitor for deep fatigue. If the PM session feels excessively hard despite being Zone 1/2, skip it.

## 10. Heat and Altitude Adaptation Notes
*   **Heat Adaptation:**
    *   Takes 7-14 days. Core temperature must be elevated to trigger adaptation (increased blood plasma volume, lower sweat threshold).
    *   *Protocol:* Ride indoors without a fan (or in extra layers) for 60-90 minutes at Zone 2. Do this 3-4 times a week leading up to a hot event.
    *   *Warning:* Do NOT do high-intensity intervals during heat adaptation protocols; power output will be suppressed and recovery delayed.
*   **Altitude Adaptation:**
    *   Takes 14-21 days for full acclimatization (increased red blood cell mass).
    *   *Protocol:* Arrive either immediately before the event (within 24 hours, before altitude sickness sets in) or at least 14 days prior.
    *   *Training at Altitude:* Power output will drop (approx. 1% per 1000ft above 3000ft). Lower your FTP setting on your head unit. Focus on perceived exertion (RPE) rather than strict power numbers for the first 5-7 days. Hydrate aggressively, as high altitude air is dry.
